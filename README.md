# Adoption & Change Management Scoping Assistant

## Purpose
This AI-powered prompt helps you conduct live scoping conversations with customers, providing real-time analysis, identifying gaps, and generating actionable recommendations for adoption and change management readiness.

---

## How to Use This Prompt

1. **Copy the entire prompt** from `Adoption_Scoping_Prompt.md` (everything from "PROMPT START" to "PROMPT END")
2. **Paste it into your AI assistant** (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.) at the start of your customer meeting
3. **The AI will guide you section-by-section** through the conversation
4. **After each section:**
   - Share what the customer said
   - AI analyzes responses and identifies gaps
   - AI asks clarifying questions for you to ask the customer
   - You gather additional info and confirm when ready to move on
5. **At the end**, receive a comprehensive readiness assessment with specific recommendations

---

## What the AI Does

The AI will guide you through 10 critical assessment areas:

1. **Project & Change Scope** - Understanding the change and impact
2. **Executive Sponsorship & Leadership** - Leadership commitment and resources
3. **Organizational Change Capacity** - Bandwidth and change fatigue
4. **Stakeholder Analysis & Engagement** - Key players and resistance points
5. **Change Management Capabilities** - Internal expertise and planning
6. **Training & Enablement Readiness** - Learning strategies and measurement
7. **Communication Strategy** - Multi-channel messaging and feedback
8. **Cultural & Organizational Context** - Culture and trust levels
9. **Success Metrics & Measurement** - How adoption will be tracked
10. **Risk Assessment** - Barriers, dependencies, and mitigation

For each section, the AI will:
- Present questions to ask
- Analyze customer responses
- Identify strengths, gaps, and red flags
- Suggest clarifying questions to fill gaps
- Confirm readiness before moving to the next section

## Tips for Using During Live Conversations

### Before the Meeting
- Paste the prompt into your AI assistant
- Have it ready in a separate window/screen
- Brief the customer that you'll be taking notes throughout

### During the Meeting
- **Read questions naturally** - Don't read verbatim; make them conversational
- **Take notes or copy-paste** responses into the AI
- **Weave in clarifying questions** naturally when AI suggests them
- Use phrases like "That's helpful - can you tell me more about..." or "I want to make sure I understand..."

### Managing the Flow
- **Don't rush** - It's okay to spend more time on areas with gaps
- **Capture tangents** but redirect to stay on track
- **Reference your framework** - You can say you're checking your notes to ensure comprehensive coverage
- **Stay flexible** - Mark sections complete but be ready to circle back if needed

### After Each Section
- Briefly review the AI's analysis
- Ask any critical clarifying questions before moving on
- Summarize: "Let me make sure I captured that correctly..."

## Common Scenarios & How to Handle

| Scenario | How to Handle |
|----------|---------------|
| **Customer doesn't know** | Note it as a gap; AI will flag it in the assessment |
| **Customer is vague** | AI will suggest probing questions to get specifics |
| **Customer is overly optimistic** | AI will identify this and suggest reality-checking questions |
| **Running out of time** | Ask AI which sections are most critical given what you've learned so far |
| **Customer goes off-topic** | Capture useful info, then gently redirect to the framework |
| **Need to circle back** | It's okay to jump around - note what needs follow-up |

## Best Practices

1. **Start with context** - Always provide the AI with customer and contact information first
2. **Be thorough** - Better to over-share customer responses than under-share
3. **Trust the process** - Let the AI guide you through all sections systematically

5. **Follow up on gaps** - If critical info is missing, schedule follow-up conversations
6. **Share the assessment** - Use AI output as basis for proposals, statements of work, or project plans
