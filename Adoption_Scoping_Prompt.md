### Section 1: Project & Change Scope

**Core Discovery Questions:**

1. **Change Type & Magnitude:**
   - What is the fundamental nature of this change? (technology adoption | process redesign | organizational restructuring | culture shift | policy change)
   - On a scale of 1-10, how disruptive will this be to daily work? What drives that rating?

2. **Impact Footprint:**
   - Which user groups are affected? (specific roles, departments, locations, employee count per segment)
   - Are there power users or critical roles disproportionately impacted?

3. **Behavioral Transformation Required:**
   - What specific behaviors must change? (tools used | work processes | collaboration patterns | decision-making authority | reporting structures)
   - What familiar practices will users need to abandon?

4. **Implementation Timeline:**
   - What is the planned rollout schedule? (pilot dates | phased rollout | big bang | timeline flexibility)
   - What is driving the timeline? (business imperative | contract deadline | competitive pressure | arbitrary date)

5. **Value Proposition:**
   - What problem does this solve for end-users? (efficiency | capability | compliance | strategic alignment)
   - What's the "WIIFM" (What's In It For Me) for impacted users?

**Analysis Framework:**
After receiving responses, evaluate:
- **Scope Clarity:** Is the change well-defined or ambiguous?
- **Impact Assessment:** Has the organization quantified the disruption?
- **Timeline Realism:** Does the timeline align with change complexity and organizational capacity?
- **User Value:** Is there a compelling end-user benefit or is this change imposed?
- **Evidence Gaps:** What critical details are missing or need validation?

### Section 2: Executive Sponsorship & Leadership

**Core Discovery Questions:**

1. **Sponsor Identification & Authority:**
   - Who is the executive sponsor? (name, title, organizational level: C-suite | VP | Director)

   - What governance structure exists? (steering committee | change advisory board | project management office)

**Analysis Framework:**
Evaluate using the **Prosci Sponsorship Model**:
- **Active & Visible:** Does the sponsor demonstrate public commitment?
- **Building Coalition:** Is the sponsor rallying other leaders?
- **Communicating Directly:** Does the sponsor personally deliver key messages?
- **Demonstrating Buy-in:** Does the sponsor model the new behaviors?
- **Removing Barriers:** Does the sponsor actively address resistance and obstacles?

**Red Flags:**
- Sponsor is too junior to influence all impacted areas
- "Supportive but passive" leadership (sponsorship by delegation)
- No dedicated change management budget
- Vague or missing governance structure
- Sponsor cannot articulate the business case

## Tips for Using This Prompt During Live Conversations

**Before the meeting:**
- Paste the prompt into your AI assistant
- Have it ready in a separate window/screen
- Brief the customer that you'll be taking notes throughout

**During the meeting:**
- Read questions naturally (don't read verbatim - make them conversational)
- Take notes or copy-paste responses into the AI
- When the AI asks clarifying questions, weave them naturally into the conversation
- Use phrases like "That's helpful - can you tell me more about..." or "I want to make sure I understand..."

**Managing the flow:**
- Don't rush - it's okay to spend more time on areas with gaps
- If the customer goes off on tangents, capture useful info and redirect
- You can tell the customer you're checking your notes/framework to ensure you cover everything
- Mark sections complete in your mind but be flexible to circle back

**After each section:**
- Briefly review the AI's analysis
- Ask any critical clarifying questions before moving on
- You can say "Let me make sure I captured that correctly..." and summarize

**Common scenarios:**
- **Customer doesn't know** - Note it as a gap; AI will flag it
- **Customer is vague** - AI will suggest probing questions
- **Customer is overly optimistic** - AI will identify this and suggest reality-checking questions
- **You're running out of time** - Ask AI which sections are most critical given what you've learned
